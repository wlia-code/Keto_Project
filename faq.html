FAQs

What is the ketogenic diet?

A high-fat, low-carbohydrate diet designed to put the body in a state of ketosis, where it burns fat for fuel instead of carbohydrates

What are the benefits of following a ketogenic diet?

Some benefits include weight loss, improved insulin sensitivity, increased energy levels, and reduced risk of chronic diseases.

Can the ketogenic diet be harmful?

The diet can be harmful if not followed correctly, leading to nutrient deficiencies, dehydration, and increased risk of liver and kidney problems.

What are the macronutrient ratios for a ketogenic diet?

Typically 70-75% of calories come from fat, 20-25% from protein, and 5-10% from carbohydrates.

What foods are allowed on a ketogenic diet?

Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, low-carb vegetables, cheese, butter, oils, and some nuts and seeds.

What foods are restricted on a ketogenic diet?

High-carb foods such as bread, pasta, rice, sugar, fruit, and starchy vegetables.

How long does it take to get into ketosis?

It varies, but it typically takes 1-2 days to a few weeks to get into ketosis.

How can I measure if I am in ketosis?

You can measure ketones in your blood, urine, or breath using ketone test strips, a blood glucose meter, or a breath analyzer.

Can I exercise on a ketogenic diet?

Yes, exercise is recommended and can be done while following a ketogenic diet.

How long should I follow a ketogenic diet?

It is recommended to follow the diet for short periods, under the supervision of a healthcare professional, as a long-term, strict adherence can lead to health issues.
